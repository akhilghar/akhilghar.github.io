Braking Systems Engineer

Texas Guadaloop - Emergency Pneumatic Braking System Design

Overview

As a braking systems engineer, I designed an emergency braking system that halts the pod in any failure event, regardless of hazard type. The architecture uses pneumatic logic so that failure points across the pod are reflected directly in brake-system pressure behavior, triggering brake caliper actuation and stopping the vehicle.

With this design, I achieved a braking distance of 0.4 meters from a top speed of 5 m/s.

Piping and Instrumentation Diagram (P&ID)

The calipers are spring-loaded, which means the brakes actuate when insufficient air pressure is present. Pressure transducers detect abnormal pressure values and flag failures within the braking system. Normally open electronically actuated vents and valves then enable immediate caliper engagement.

P&ID of the fail-safe pneumatic braking architecture.

Wheel Assembly Integration

The wheel assembly was designed to integrate directly with the braking hardware while maintaining packaging constraints and serviceability. The geometry supports robust braking contact and reliable operation under dynamic loads.

Wheel assembly integrated with the pneumatic braking system.

Prototype Pneumatic Braking Assembly

A full prototype was built to validate pneumatic routing, valve response, and fail-safe behavior in representative operating conditions.

Prototype assembly used for functional validation and testing.
