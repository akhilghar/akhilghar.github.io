About

Welcome to my portfolio! I am a current second-year mechanical engineering student at the University of Texas at Austin. Ever since I was a kid, I had been fascinated by two things in life: numbers and Legos. When I discovered mechanical engineering as a career path, I realized it was a perfect mesh of my two biggest interests in life, compelling me to explore this new world. After getting into UT, I began to see the true versatility of mechanical engineering: people with backgrounds ranging from materials science to aeronautical vehicles flooded the faculty and research facilities. While this versatility makes it hard for me to learn everything the engineering world has to offer, I still hope to acquire as much knowledge and application as possible.

My goal as a mechanical engineer is to ultimately develop autonomous technologies to service individual needs. The field of robotics and automation is utterly fascinating, as it has evolved from mere robotic arms to several forms, ranging from airborne drones to planetary rovers. I hope to drive further innovations in this industry with a focus on improving the daily lives of humans everywhere, maintaining the comfort factor while our lives keep accelerating.
